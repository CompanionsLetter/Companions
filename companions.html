Note from the Author

Temptations - the easy way out

Temptations are the voices in our head saying "There are easy ways for you to feel happy", "You can escape reality by doing something you love doing, isn't that great?" (Speaking about spiritual temptations, being tempted to eat a candy might not be that bad unless it's gluttony)

Lies of the enemy. When you're tempted by the enemy, he offers you easy ways out, ways to make you happy, to make you forget about your sadness, bad situation, or stress.

We are mostly tempted when we are vulnerable:

  • We had a bad day at school or work
  • We had a bad interaction with family or friends
  • We got mad at ourselves
  • We started overthinking about all the worse things that happened or might happen

Falling into temptation feels good for the moment, your stress goes away, you feel happy for a bit, but after you fall into it and you realize what you've done - the stress goes away... and guilt appears.

What is worse than stress? Stress is mostly directed to the future or present. Guilt is directed to the past - you cannot change the past.

So after you fall into temptation, after you fall, you're filled with guilt:

  • "I told God I wouldn't do it, yet I still did it."
  • "I've done this sin so much already"
  • "Why can I never flee this sin"

On the bright side

It's good that you feel guilt, it means you seek to actually stop sinning.

On the darker side

Too much guilt destroys you, thinking about how stupid you are and how foolish for falling into temptation for the millionth time won't help you.

Pray and ask for forgiveness, reflect on your actions, understand your triggers, understand your bad choices and find solutions for your temptation problem.

How to Fight Temptation

One of the best ways to fight temptation is not to fight it directly, but to flee from it, to run from it.

Spiritually run from it, and not just spiritually, literally physically run away from it - You are feeling tempted, run away from the place you are and go in a place where there's more people or just go wash your face, pray to God, don't let yourself near the place where you were tempted.

When you are tempted, you sometimes just flirt with it, you flirt with sin, and you don't fully realize what you are doing, the enemy tries to blind you so that you are vulnerable and not aware.

You need to realize what's happening, you need to be aware of your thoughts and actions - When you get tempted, say your thoughts out loud: "I want to sin".

Now you've acknowledged the temptation, after telling yourself what you want to do, tell God, and ask him for help so that you may not do that.

My Personal Experience

In my own experience, I've always fallen into temptation when I either was numb - I filled myself with distractions, I ran away from every emotion and I was just on autopilot, so when I was tempted, I just fell into it because I didn't have any emotion, I just searched for easy pleasure and happiness.

I also fell when I started flirting with the sin - the more you let it stay in your mind, the more it grows, from 1 single evil spirit, 7 appear, 10, 50, 100, as many needed to make you fall - you need to act fast: Acknowledge the sin - say it out loud - say it to God.

I also fell when I had TOO many feelings in my head, mostly bad feelings, like worry, stress, sadness - I was so sad that temptation sounded good, it sounded like a moment of happiness, it sounded like escape from reality, that's exactly what I needed when feeling stressed, but the truth is that after you sin the stress is still there, the sadness, and now there's even guilt too.

"When the unclean spirit has gone out of a person, it passes through waterless places seeking rest, but finds none. Then it says, 'I will return to my house from which I came.' And when it comes, it finds the house empty, swept, and put in order. Then it goes and brings with it seven other spirits more evil than itself, and they enter and dwell there, and the last state of that person is worse than the first."

Matthew 12:43-45

The temptation is not gone yet, it will always come back even more strong. And if you just think you've won, you will forget about it and you will be vulnerable again. But you need to be extremely careful, for the enemy is like a roaring lion waiting to strike you.

Never forget about the times when you were tempted, pray to God for protection and STAY AWARE.

A Recent Example

Today I was tempted too, I realized that, I acknowledged the temptation, why did I get tempted? I had a pretty bad day, I was stressing, worrying and I felt disappointed.

Getting some easy happiness sounded good, but instead of giving up those emotions of stress, worry, disappointment, I ABSORBED THEM, I acknowledged them too, I realized that I'm a human, it's okay for me to make mistakes, it's okay for me to worry about certain things.

So I stayed with those emotions, but I also tried to think positively, so that those feelings won't always be that bad, and I also didn't try to run from those emotions by falling into temptation.

I went to sleep, I took a nap so I don't think or flirt with the temptations.

Choosing nap instead of sin? It might look small, but biblically, that's resisting the devil (James 4:7). And what does the verse say next?

"Resist the devil, and he will flee from you"

You're not the one who has to flee forever. When you resist enough, he flees.

Each time you don't run from your emotions, each time you don't flirt with sin, each time you acknowledge your thoughts and you give them onto God - even doing it halfway, the temptation loses a bit of its grip.

"Resist the devil, and he will flee from you",

James 4:7
